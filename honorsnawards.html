Best Entrepreneurship Award

Issued by National Kaohsiung University of Science and Technology · Nov 2020

Since I have always been very curious about entrepreneurship, when I was a freshman, I signed up for the Entrepreneurship Competition of the University of Science and Technology of China. This is a competition where you must come up with a feasible entrepreneurial idea and make a presentation on stage within 72 hours. I chose to use Taiwan's aging society as a pain point and proposed the entrepreneurial idea of ​​a smart scooter. After countless discussions and revisions, our team finally successfully proposed a convincing entrepreneurial concept and stood out in the competition and won the Best Entrepreneurship Award (a bonus of 5,000 NTD).

This competition taught me how to create a persuasive brief, how to effectively convince potential consumers to buy our product, and how to highlight the unique benefits of the product. More importantly, the experience emphasized the importance of creativity and persistence. Ideas should never be limited and we should continue to innovate and work tirelessly to achieve our goals. This competition also wrote an important first chapter for my entrepreneurial journey and inspired me to continuously pursue innovative and entrepreneurial opportunities to realize my dreams.
